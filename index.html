About me

I hold a PhD and MSci in Mathematics from the University of Bristol for which I have obtained numerous awards. My speciality is Mathematical Physics within which I have enjoyed the success of creating and publishing new mathematics. I enjoy complementing my academic career with an active lifestyle including rock climbing, surfing and cycling.

Teaching experience

During my eight years studying at Bristol I helped pioneer a new tutoring format to help undergraduate mathematics students. This stepped away from the traditional presentation style to an interactive form lead by the student's questions, learning styles and interests. Since 2011 I have built on these highly successful and flexible techniques to additionally provide A-level tuition and have been impressed by the results.

Approach to tutoring

Tutoring allows the unique opportunity for a student to engage with a particular topic, syllabus or the wider application of a subject in a way best suited to their individual needs. I aim to tailor my tutorials to best take advantage of this, based on what the student is looking to gain from each tutoring session. This could include discussing a method, working through questions together, planning and revising for an examination, guided note writing or simply informally answering questions the student has.
